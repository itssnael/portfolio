Concert poster

About the project

This project was carried out during my first-year MMI internship at LIFE TV under the supervision and guidance of my internship mentor. The objective was to create a poster inspired by one or more existing posters, analyzing their design process and adapting them to my own style. I also had to adapt the poster into an Instagram story and a Facebook post.

The Process

I decided to create a concert poster for the artist Tyla. I first selected a photo of Tyla and cut it out using Photoshop. Then, I chose the colors, a pattern, fonts, and created elements in Illustrator to design my poster. Finally, I adapted my visual to different formats for social media.

Brand Guide

About the project

This project is a brand guide based on the theme of Old Hollywood for the design of Vanier College’s 2025 Yearbook.

The Process

My team and I first defined the theme and style we wanted for our concept, then created a mood board. Next, we selected the colors and typography for our designs before assembling the elements to create the layout for the student profile pages in the Yearbook.

Graphic Book

About the project

This project is a graphic book inspired by the South Korean artist So Youn Lee, showcasing writing exercises completed as part of my MMI studies.

The Process

To achieve this, I first selected the artist who inspired me the most, as well as the texts I wanted to illustrate. I then analyzed So Youn Lee’s works to determine how to incorporate her graphic elements into my project. Finally, I designed and created this book in InDesign.

Rage

About the project

This project is a visual identity creation. I designed RAGE, an innovative brand aiming to establish itself in the boxing equipment market.

The Process

To achieve this, I first defined my brand’s activity, analyzed competitors, and determined its philosophy. Then, from the original photo, I hand-drew four variations of the tiger’s head, scanned the final sketch, and vectorized it using Illustrator. Once this step was completed, I selected a typography for the logo, tested several options, and finalized the vectorization.
